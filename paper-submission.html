Authors Guidelines:


Guidelines

For each accepted paper, at least one author is required to register and present the work at the conference. Authors are invited to submit original manuscripts of 8–10 pages in A4 size, prepared according to the prescribed template. A standard registration covers one accepted paper.

Paper Submission Guidelines:


An International Conference On Emerging Technologies Innovation And Sustainable Development (ICETIS-2026) will be initiated by Dr. Ashok Gujar Technical Institute’s , Dr. Daulatrao Aher College of Engineering Karad, India and CTIF Global Capsule (CGC), Denmark Kuttervej 13, 9990 Skagen, Denmark on 23rd and 24th March 2026. The initiative aims to promote high‐quality academic exchange, innovation-driven research, and global collaboration in emerging areas of science, engineering, technology, and sustainable development. ICETIS-2026 invites authors to submit their original and unpublished work that demonstrates current research in all areas of Emerging Technologies and sustainable development on mail id- icetis-2026@dacoe.ac.in . Acceptance of papers will be communicated to authors by email.

Manuscript Preparation:


• Manuscripts must be prepared using the prescribed MS Word or LaTeX template.

• All figures must be high quality (minimum 600 dpi), and tables must not be submitted as images.

• All content must remain within the page margins.

• The full affiliation, address, and email ID of all authors must be provided, and one author must be designated as the corresponding author with a valid email address.

• Manuscripts must be written in clear, grammatically correct English.

• All references, figures, and tables must be numbered sequentially and cited in the text.

• Submissions not complying with these guidelines will be rejected without review.

Policy on Plagiarism:


• Authors must strictly avoid plagiarism in all its forms.

• Submissions should be original, unpublished research work and must not be under consideration for publication elsewhere.

• Manuscripts found to contain plagiarized material at any stage of the review process will be rejected.

• As per the copyright transfer agreement, authors are individually and collectively responsible for the content of their manuscripts.

• Each author is expected to uphold the highest ethical standards with respect to originality and plagiarism.
